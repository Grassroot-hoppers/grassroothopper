02 — THE PROBLEM

Your city is full of artists you'll never find.

The person you dance with every Thursday is a painter. The guy from your coworking space writes poetry. Your neighbor runs a gallery. You had no idea. Instagram buries them. Facebook forgot them. The platforms that promised connection delivered feeds, ads, and strangers.

03 — THE IDEA

Join through a community you already belong to.

Your dance scene. Your art class. Your dog park. Scan a QR code. Say a secret password — spoken in person, never written down. Discover the hidden creative lives of the people you already know.

04 — HOW IT WORKS

Five steps. That's it.

  1. Scan a QR code at a community event
  2. Say the secret password — spoken in person, never written down
  3. Choose: reader or creator
  4. See only familiar faces — every person is someone you already know
  5. Walk up and say it: "I love your last pictures."
Technically: blogs behind a gate with an event calendar. That's the entire product.
05 — THE COUNTERCULTURE

Everything we removed.

NO

  • Comments
  • Likes
  • DMs
  • Algorithm
  • Follower counts
  • Notifications
  • Contact info
  • Digital reach

YES

  • Your work, seen by people who know your face
  • Events in one place
  • Familiar faces only
  • Real feedback — someone walks up and says it
06 — YOU OWN IT

Not a user. A co-owner.

Each city's instance is a cooperative (SC). Members vote by role. Creators hold 40% of votes. Readers 20%. Event planners 30%. Devs 10%. Your name is on the register. You vote on decisions. You share in the value.

€5/month for creators. Free for readers. No ads. No investors. No exit strategy.

07 — THE SECRET

I want to be with those who know secret things or else alone.

— Rainer Maria Rilke

The password is never written down. Not in any app. Not in any message. It is only spoken. At events. In person. That's the grassroot way to make sure no bots get inside.

08 — BRUSSELS

Launching with Lindy Hop Brussels.

A tight group of dancers who are also painters, singers, poets, and photographers. Building the community before building the technology. The first QR code goes up at a social.

This is the first demo. Help us build it.
