It always starts with one person.

One passionate person forks the code and starts with their own community. From there, it grows organically. Bottom up. Always.

♥
It starts here
One person. One community.
Someone who cares forks the repo. They don't need permission, funding, or a team. They start with the community they already belong to — the one where they know faces, show up to events, and feel at home.
💃 Lindy Hop Brussels
❖
Organic growth
Word spreads. More communities join.
People in that first community belong to other communities too. They see it works. They say: "We should do this for our group." Each new hub starts the same way — someone who cares, a QR code, a spoken password.

Maturity
Hubs mature into a city-wide cooperative.
When enough communities are active, the members form a société coopérative. They choose a name. They elect governance. Creators, readers, event planners, devs — each with weighted voting power. The city now owns its own creative infrastructure.
∞
Natural divergence
One city, multiple co-ops. That's fine.
If members feel the need — different age groups, different vibes, different neighborhoods — multiple cooperatives can coexist in the same city. No one decides this from the top. The community decides by doing. Fork again. Start again. The code is open.
The same pattern, everywhere.
Every city starts the same way: one person who cares, one community they already belong to, one fork of the repo. The rest is organic.

No central authority decides how many co-ops exist in a city. If a group of members feels they want a different vibe — younger crowd, different language, neighborhood-focused — they fork and start their own. The city becomes a garden, not a franchise. Multiple co-ops can share the same open-source tools and even federate if they choose. Or not. That's up to them.

Fork, don't ask

Coopyleft license. Any cooperative or non-profit can fork and launch. No permission, no franchise fee, no headquarters.

The handbook

Federate, don't centralize
