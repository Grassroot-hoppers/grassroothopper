01

The people who make your city interesting are invisible to each other.

You dance with someone every Thursday. You don't know she's a painter. The guy at your coworking space writes poetry. Your neighbor runs a gallery. Instagram buries them. Spotify takes a cut. Facebook shows you 30% of events.

02

You join through a community you already belong to.

Your dance scene. Your art class. Your running club. Your dog park. Through that community, you discover the hidden creative lives of the people you already know.

Not strangers discovering strangers — but people who already share space discovering each other.

03

Five steps. That's it.

1

Scan a QR code at a community event.

2

Say the secret password — spoken in person. Never written down. You were there. That's your credential.

3

Choose: reader or creator. Readers discover. Creators share their work.

4

See only familiar faces. Every person is someone you already know. Some of them are opera singers. Poets. Illustrators.

5

Walk up and say it. “I love your last pictures.” No comment button. No heart emoji. Real words, face to face.

04

What we don't build

Comments Likes DMs Feeds Followers Notifications Payments Algorithms

If you want to tell someone their work is amazing, you tell them in person.

Technically: blogs behind a gate with an event calendar.
That's the entire product.

05

“I want to be alone. I want to be with people who know secret things or are also alone.”

— Rainer Maria Rilke, Letters to a Young Poet

A secret password, spoken in person. Never written down. Not in any app. Not in any message. You were there — that's the only credential that matters.

06

You're not a user.
You're a co-owner.

Each city's Grassroot Hopper is a cooperative. Members vote by role: creators, readers, event planners, devs. The code is open source. Anyone on the planet can start their own.

Launching with the Lindy Hop community in Brussels — a tight group of dancers who are also painters, singers, poets, and photographers. Building the community before building the technology.

07

If this made your eyes light up — you're one of us.
