1
Clara posts her paintings on her blog. Nothing else. No hashtags, no promotion, no "check out my work." Just the paintings.
2
Everyone in her three communities sees her work. 304 people who already know her face. The dancer she tangos with. The runner she high-fives at the finish line. The poet she sits next to.
3
They become genuine fans. Not followers. Fans. Because they know her. Next time they see her, they say it: "I love your last paintings."
4
A gallery invites Clara to exhibit. She posts about the exhibition on her blog.
5
Every community lights up. The runners see it. The dancers see it. The poetry group sees it. "One of our members is exhibiting at Gallery X!"
6
The gallery fills with visitors who actually care. Not strangers who clicked an ad. Real people with a real connection.
7
The gallery is thrilled. Clara is thrilled. The community members feel proud. Nobody paid for an ad. Nobody gamed an algorithm.

The network creates value through real human connections — not algorithms.

People in real communities naturally amplify the creative lives of people they already know. That's the mechanism. That's the flywheel.
