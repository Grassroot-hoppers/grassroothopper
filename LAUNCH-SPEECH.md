# The Launch Speech — Lindy Hop Brussels

*Julien's speech at a Lindy Hop social. The moment Grassroot Hopper becomes real.*

---

Thank you for your attention. It's so nice that you're all here tonight.

Now, as you already know — we are all gathered, and we gather most of the time, and you see similar faces all the time. You might know that some of the people you dance with are artists, creators, writers, poets, photographers. But maybe you find it hard to connect with that side of them.

What I have in my hand is a QR code.

It's a QR code for the Lindy Hop community of Brussels. Scan it. Choose a username. Upload your photo if you want. And decide: do you want to be a reader, or a creator?

**Readers** will have instant access to all the people inside the Lindy Hop community who have decided to be a creator.

**Creators** — each of you tonight who wants to start showing the Lindy Hop community what you do — can scan this QR code, sign up as a creator, and start posting on your own personal portfolio. Your art. Your thoughts. Like a personal blog.

As you scan the QR code and enter this Lindy Hop community hub, you will see the list of all the other people who show up to practice and who decided to be brave enough to share their portfolio.

As you scroll through the content of this community — you will only see the work of familiar faces. People you already dance with.

And you would be surprised to learn that some of you are opera singers. House battle dancers. Poets. Illustrators.

Maybe you are also an illustrator. And you've been dancing with your next collaborator without knowing.

This is the Grassroot Hopper project.

A social network. Ultra-local. By the people, for the people. Where you only see content from the people you already like.

This may inspire you to show your own work. Or to start your own craft. And next time you go dance, you can walk up to someone and say: "I love your last pictures. I love the way you sing. I wish you could tell me more about it."

That's true connection. The promise of social networks that never arrived.

---

*[Hold up QR code]*
