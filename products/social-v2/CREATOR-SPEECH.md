# The Creator Speech — How It Works for You

*The companion to the Launch Speech. Not for a stage — for a conversation. One creator talking to another at a social activity.*

---

Don't spend time on social media. Spend time on social activities.

As you do social activities — dancing, workshops, creative workshops, an art class, a reading club, a running club — you create relationships in groups. Real ones. At some point, you mention it: "Hey, we could launch our own Grassroots Hopper community. I can set it up for you. I'll give you a QR code, and we're on this new social network. It's only for us."

That's how it starts. Not an app download. Not a sign-up form. A conversation at an event.

---

Here's what happens when you set up a community.

You're a creator. You post your art, your writing, your photos, your music — whatever you make. The members of your community see it. Not because you shared it. Not because you posted a link. Just because they're in the same community as you. They see your work organically, the way you'd see a friend's painting on their wall when you visit.

And here's the thing — they can also see the other communities you're part of. You're in the running club, but you're also in the Lindy Hop community. If someone in the running club thinks you're cool, they look at your profile and see: "Oh, she's also in Lindy Hop Brussels." They can't subscribe to that community from the app. They can't browse and join. To enter a community, you need two things:

**A QR code. And a secret password.**

The password is managed by the community. Changed when the community decides to change it. Voted on by the members. And here's the rule: the password is never written down. Not in any app. Not in any WhatsApp group. Not in any email. It is only spoken. At events. In person.

That's the grassroot way to make sure no AI bots get inside the community. No scrapers. No fake accounts. No noise. Just people who were physically present when the password was shared.

---

*"I want to be with those who know secret things or else alone."*
— Rainer Maria Rilke

That's the brand. A secret message to enter a community you deserve to be part of.

---

Now, back to you as a creator. The incentive is simple.

You create communities for the groups you live in. You start running with a group — you propose a running community. You join an art class — you set one up. Every community you're part of, every community you help build, makes your creative work visible to more people. Not strangers. People who know you.

And the system notices.

A creator who is part of multiple communities, who administers a community, who has members and interaction — that person is naturally recognized as a community leader. Not by an algorithm. By the simple fact that real people in real communities chose to be around them.

Now imagine you're an event organizer. You're looking for an illustrator for your next project. You look at the platform and you see: this woman is an artist. She's part of four communities. She administers one of them. She's loved by the people around her. She has 500 people who actually know her and like her work.

Not 10,000 followers who might be bots. Not noise. Five hundred real people who live in this city and would show up if she's involved.

If you make an event with her, you're going to get 500 real customers on the first day. So you hire her. Not because she promoted herself. Because the community made her visible.

That's the creator's incentive. You don't market yourself. You live your life. You create communities for the groups you belong to. And your work finds its audience — because the audience already knows your face.
