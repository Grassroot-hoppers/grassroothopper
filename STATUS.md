## One-liner

A cooperative, ultra-local platform connecting Brussels' creative people — artists, musicians, writers, photographers — with normal people who want to find them, follow them, and meet them in real life.

## Current phase

Brainstorming. Defining the vision. Learning about the landscape (platform cooperativism, Transition Towns model, existing cooperative platforms). **Not committing to a specific product yet.**

## Key decisions made

- **Cooperative ownership.** The platform is owned by its members, not investors.
- **No direct selling.** Creators cannot sell directly on the platform. This keeps incentives clean. Flagging mechanism for too much commercialization.
- **Newsletter-first.** The MVP is a network of passionate local newsletter writers, not an app.
- **Ultra-local.** Brussels first. Not a global platform. People you can meet on the street.
- **AI as infrastructure, not product.** AI handles the boring stuff (distribution, matching, formatting). Humans do the connecting. 20-40% AI, 60-80% real human work.
- **Open-source and forkable.** Other cities can pick up the software and run their own version. CoopCycle/Decidim model.
- **Non-closed exploration.** The final product might be different from the current vision. The process matters more than the destination right now.

## The non-closed action plan

The point is to start moving in the field, talking to people, and discovering what this actually needs to be — without locking in a specific product.

### Month 1-2: Listen (March–April 2026)

**Have 10 coffee conversations with creative people in Brussels.**

Not pitching. Not recruiting. Just asking one question: *"How do people find you? How do you wish they found you?"*

Target list to build:
- [ ] 2-3 visual artists (ask Lucia for introductions)
- [ ] 1-2 musicians (Lindy Hop scene, jazz scene)
- [ ] 1-2 event organizers (workshop organizers, gallery owners)
- [ ] 1-2 writers or bloggers already writing about Brussels
- [ ] 1-2 photographers
- [ ] 1 person running a local cultural space or venue

**What to capture from each conversation:**
- How they currently get visibility (Instagram? Word of mouth? Nothing?)
- What frustrates them about the current tools
- Do they know other creatives? How connected is the scene?
- Would they write a short newsletter about their passion? What would it take?
- What would make them join something new?

### Month 2-3: One newsletter that isn't mine (April–May 2026)

Find *one* person in Brussels who is passionate about a local topic and help them write their first newsletter. Not on a fancy platform. On Ghost, Substack, anything. Do it *with* them, not for them.

Observe:
- Do readers come?
- Do they share it?
- Does the writer enjoy it?
- What's the natural friction?

### Month 3-4: The first gathering (May–June 2026)

A physical event. Small. A drink somewhere in Brussels.

- 5 newsletter writers (or aspiring ones)
- 5 artists/creatives
- 10 readers/normal people who want to discover local creative life

No app. No platform. Just: "Here are people who write about Brussels. Here are people who make things in Brussels. Here are people who want to find both."

See what happens in the room.

### After that: decide what to build

By then you'll know:
- Is there real demand?
- What do people actually need (vs. what I imagine they need)?
- Is this a platform, a newsletter network, a cooperative, a physical event series, or something else entirely?
- Who are the first 3-5 people who would co-build this?

## Key insight

> The final product might not be what I imagine today. But the process of talking to artists, creatives, and normal people in Brussels about how they find each other — that process has value no matter what it leads to. The worst outcome is that I meet interesting people and understand my city better.

## Key decisions made about AI

- AI is 20-40% of the work. Not more.
- AI will never replace collaborating with smart, deterministic people who have opinions.
- The trap is delegating thinking to AI. The work is in the field.
- Collection of simple AI tools > one complex AI system.
- AI makes it possible to run this with a tiny team and low costs.

## Reference models

| Model | What they proved | Link |
|---|---|---|
| **Transition Towns** (Rob Hopkins) | Grassroots can scale globally with a handbook, a network, and passionate local leaders. No corporation needed. | [transitionnetwork.org](https://transitionnetwork.org/) |
| **CoopCycle** | Open-source platform, "Coopyleft" license, federated city-by-city. Each city runs its own co-op on shared software. | [coopcycle.org](https://coopcycle.org/) |
| **Decidim** | Barcelona built it, open-sourced it, now cities worldwide fork it. Proves the "forkable city software" model. | [decidim.org](https://decidim.org/) |
| **Resonate** | Co-op music streaming. €10/year membership. Stream-to-own. Proves co-op model works for creative platforms. | [resonate.coop](https://resonate.coop/) |
| **6AM City** | 2M subscribers, 400+ US cities. Proves massive demand for hyperlocal newsletters. But ad-funded and corporate — wrong model, right proof of demand. | [6amcity.com](https://6amcity.com/) |
| **Platform Cooperativism** (Trebor Scholz) | 1.2M+ workers in 53 countries in cooperative digital economy. The intellectual framework for all of this. | [platform.coop](https://platform.coop/) |
