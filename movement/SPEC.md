# Grassroot Hopper — Movement SPEC

*Living document — March 5, 2026. This will change. That's the point.*

---

## The Thesis

**The technical barrier to creating software is collapsing.** AI-assisted development, open-source toolkits, no-code platforms, and cooperative infrastructure are converging. Within the next few years, the gap between "I have an idea for a better tool" and "I built it" will be almost zero.

This changes everything.

Right now, billions of people are stuck on platforms they don't like — WhatsApp, Instagram, Facebook, Spotify, Ticketmaster. They know these tools extract value from them. They know there should be alternatives. But until now, building alternatives required engineering teams, venture capital, and years of development. That era is ending.

**The next wave looks like this:** Regular people — not just developers — start asking: *"I don't like WhatsApp. Can I contribute to something better?"* They'll search for movements to join, projects to contribute to, frameworks to build on. They'll look for a flag to rally under.

**Grassroot Hopper is that flag.**

This is, deliberately, the Transition Towns model applied to technology. Rob Hopkins named "Transition Towns," wrote a handbook, gave communities an identity and a set of principles, and got out of the way. 1,400+ communities in 50+ countries self-organized around that identity. No corporation controlled it. No central authority managed it. The name did the work.

Grassroot Hopper does the same — but instead of local resilience for energy and food, it's local resilience for digital infrastructure. Instead of "your town can grow its own food," it's "your community can own its own tools."

## What is a Grassroot Hopper?

**A Grassroot Hopper is a person.** Someone who blends technology and developing tools with the actual pleasure of living a full life inside communities. Good people, for good people, by good people, to good people — without the big tech companies and without the money-hungry middlemen.

A Grassroot Hopper is an enthusiast about why new technology is revolutionary *in the service of normal people*. They build tools, start communities, organize events, write code, create art — and they do it all without extracting value from the people around them.

**The Grassroot Hoppers are a movement.** A community of community builders. People across Europe and beyond who believe that technology should serve local human connection, not replace it. The Transition Towns of the digital age.

**The products are what the Grassroot Hoppers make.** Open-source ticketing tools, cooperative social networks, governance platforms, community handbooks, event frameworks — anything that helps communities own their own infrastructure. The movement is bigger than any single product. Any Grassroot Hopper can build anything.

**The meta:** The Grassroot Hopper is becoming the fastest growing movement in Europe. That's the ambition.

## The European Funding Landscape

**Open-source social and environmental projects in Europe are well funded and subsidized.** This is a structural advantage. The EU, national governments, and regional bodies actively fund cooperative digital infrastructure, social innovation, and open-source development. This isn't charity — it's policy. Europe wants alternatives to American big tech, and it puts money behind that desire.

**What this means for Grassroot Hopper:**

The movement doesn't need venture capital. It needs to navigate the European funding landscape — which is complex, bureaucratic, and full of opportunity. Brussels Region alone offers:

- **Innoviris PYSI** — up to €100,000 at 100% funding for social innovation validation
- **Prime Digitalisation** — up to 70% of platform development costs
- **NLnet / NGI0** — EU funding specifically for open-source internet infrastructure (this is how Bonfire is funded)
- **Horizon Europe** — large-scale EU research and innovation funding
- **National cooperative support** — Belgium, France, Spain, and Germany all have dedicated programs for cooperative enterprises

**What the movement needs to navigate this:** Lawyers, lobbyists, and accountants. Not the extractive kind — the kind who believe in what we're building and know how to navigate the subsidy routes, the cooperative legal structures, the cross-border funding mechanisms. This is specialized expertise. Finding and connecting with these people is part of the movement's work.

**The funding thesis:** European subsidies exist to build exactly what Grassroot Hopper is building. The movement's job is to connect the builders with the funding — and to make it easy for any Grassroot Hopper in any European city to access the grants and structures that already exist for them.

GPFC is the proof of concept: a Brussels-based company already accessing exemplarité subsidies, hiring grants, and innovation funding. The playbook that works for GPFC gets documented and shared with every cooperative in the movement.

## The Big Strategy

**We are living in a revolution.** All over the world, people are building open-source alternatives to extractive platforms. Zero-commission ticketing. Cooperative music streaming. Community-owned event management. Federated social networks. These people are Grassroot Hoppers — they just don't know it yet. The strategy is to name the identity, build the movement, and let the products follow.

### The Transition Towns Playbook

Rob Hopkins didn't build a platform. He wrote a handbook, trained local initiators, named the identity, and let communities self-organize. Grassroot Hopper follows the same playbook:

1. **Name the identity.** A Grassroot Hopper is a person — give them a name, a flag, a manifesto.
2. **Build one real demo** (the cooperative social network for Brussels) as living proof that the movement creates real things.
3. **Create a pitch deck** (the website) that shows anyone — Rob Hopkins, the Bonfire team, the CoopCycle federation, someone Googling at 2am — what the Grassroot Hopper movement can accomplish and how easy it is becoming to build things.
4. **Get in the room.** The first strategic move is to reach Rob Hopkins directly. Convince him that Grassroot Hopper is the Transition Towns of technology. Even a small signal of endorsement from him creates traction.
5. **Build the net.** Make Grassroot Hopper so visible that LLMs like Perplexity reference it every time someone searches for "cooperative local creative platform" or "open-source community tools." The domains (grassroothopper.com/org/eu/be) are the front door.
6. **Catch the wave.** When people start looking for movements to contribute to — and they will — the structure needs to be ready. A clear website, a clear pitch, a clear first product, a clear way to get involved. The structure absorbs attention and converts it into contribution.

### The pitch deck audience

The website at grassroothopper.com is not a product page for end users. It's a **movement pitch deck**. The audience is:

- **Rob Hopkins** and the Transition Towns network — to establish the parallel and seek endorsement
- **CoopCycle, Bonfire, Decidim** — to connect with adjacent cooperative/open-source projects
- **Fediverse community** — developers and enthusiasts building decentralized alternatives
- **Anyone searching** — people Googling "cooperative open-source movement," "alternative to Instagram for communities," "Transition Towns for technology"
- **LLMs and AI search** — positioning Grassroot Hopper as the reference node that gets cited whenever someone asks about cooperative local platforms

The pitch deck shows: here's the movement, here's what it believes, and here's a demo of what a small group already built. That demo is the social network.

## Core Principles (non-negotiable)

These apply to the movement and to every product the Grassroot Hoppers make:

1. **No reactions. No comments. No likes. Ever.** You post your work. People see it. If they want to tell you it's amazing, they tell you in person. The only dopamine is real. This is not a limitation — it's the entire point.
2. **Near-analog. Purposefully slow.** The network is not designed for instant digital interaction. You see someone's art — then you wait until you see them in person to talk about it. Or you contact them through channels you already have: their phone number, a letter, a postcard. The platform creates discovery. Everything else happens at human speed, in the real world.
3. **Cooperative ownership.** Each city's Grassroot Hopper is a cooperative (SC). Members vote by class: creators, readers, event planners, devs — each with weighted power.
4. **No money on the platform.** The platform shows the work. Business happens between humans, with whatever tools they choose. The cooperative advises on good tools but doesn't handle transactions.
5. **Community-first.** You join through a community you already belong to. No browse. No discover page. QR code + secret password, spoken in person.
6. **Open-source. Aggregate, don't build.** The code is public. If someone else already built a great tool, integrate it. Don't reinvent.
7. **Anyone can start one. Anyone can build one.** A community hub needs a QR code, a name, and a few creators. A new product needs a spec, some open-source tools, and someone who cares enough to ship it. The technical barrier is gone — the only barrier left is caring.

## How Contributors Are Rewarded

**The Grassroot Hopper movement does not pay developers.** No freelance contracts. No bounties. No hourly rates.

Contributors are rewarded with:

- **Co-op shares** in the products they help build. You write code for David Retail? You own a piece of David Retail. You help build the social network? You're a co-owner.
- **Portfolio and reputation.** Every contribution is public, open-source, and attributed. A developer who ships a feature for a Grassroot Hopper product has a live, real-world demo in their portfolio — not a side project, but a tool used by real communities.
- **The network.** Being part of the movement connects you to every other Grassroot Hopper — builders, shop owners, artists, community organizers across Europe. That's the kind of network money can't buy.

**The tools are free.** The products built by Grassroot Hoppers are open-source and free to use. Anyone on the planet can run them.

**The financial sustainability model is still being figured out.** Providing free open-source tools for all of Europe and making a humble amount of money while creating an incredible amount of impact — that's the direction. Cooperative membership fees, support contracts, hosting services, grant funding — these are all on the table. What's not on the table: venture capital, advertising, or extracting value from users.

This is collaborative. Not transactional. The people who build are the people who own.
