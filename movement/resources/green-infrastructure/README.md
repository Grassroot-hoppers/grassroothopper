# Green Infrastructure & Waste-Heat Computing

## What it is

A growing ecosystem of European companies and initiatives rethinking where servers live and what happens to their heat. Instead of dumping waste heat into the atmosphere, these projects capture it and pipe it into homes, greenhouses, and district heating grids. The best ones co-locate servers directly in the buildings that need warmth.

## What Grassroots Hopper learns from it

**The infrastructure layer can be grassroots too.** If a community cooperative needs compute, it doesn't have to rent from hyperscalers. Companies like Leafcloud and Blockheating prove that small-scale, locally embedded data centers work — and they can be net-positive for the neighborhood.

**Heat reuse is a community benefit, not just a green talking point.** When servers heat apartment buildings (Leafcloud) or greenhouses (Blockheating), the infrastructure becomes something neighbors actually want nearby. This is a powerful argument for embedding tech into community life — the Grassroots Hopper thesis in hardware form.

**Open reference designs exist.** The Net Zero Innovation Hub and ReUseHeat project have published open toolkits and calculators. If a Grassroots Hopper cooperative ever needs to think about hosting, these are the starting blueprints.

**This connects to the "anyone can start one" principle.** If the computing infrastructure itself can be small, local, and cooperatively owned, the full stack — from hardware to software to community — can be grassroots.

## Projects

### Leafcloud
Dutch carbon-negative public cloud provider. Installs servers in apartment complexes and care homes, uses residual heat to replace natural gas for residential heating.
- https://leaf.cloud

### Qarnot Computing
French startup offering HPC by placing microprocessors inside buildings as digital heaters. Eliminates the need for server cooling entirely.
- https://qarnot.com

### Blockheating
Dutch startup providing bare-metal edge data centers in containers next to commercial greenhouses. Liquid cooling transfers waste heat directly to agriculture.
- https://blockheating.com

### Cloud&Heat Technologies
German company building water-cooled data centers that capture waste heat and feed it into municipal heating grids.
- https://www.cloudandheat.com

### Stockholm Data Parks
Swedish initiative partnering with government and industry to capture waste heat from data centers to warm residential homes across Stockholm.
- https://stockholmdataparks.com

### Helen & Equinix Waste Heat Project
Helsinki infrastructure project where energy company Helen captures excess heat from Equinix data centers for the city's district heating network.
- https://www.helen.fi

### Net Zero Innovation Hub for Data Centers
European collaborative initiative providing open reference designs and heat reuse calculators for data center decarbonization.
- https://www.netzerodatacenters.com

### ReUseHeat
EU-funded project that demonstrated modular systems for recovering urban excess heat — including from data centers — across several European cities.
- https://www.reuseheat.eu
