# Perplexity Pro Prompt — Grassroots Hopper Vision Research

*Copy-paste this into Perplexity Pro with Deep Research enabled.*

---

I'm building a concept called **Grassroots Hopper** — a cooperative, ultra-local platform connecting creative people (artists, musicians, writers, photographers, workshop organizers, poets) with normal people in their city who want to discover, follow, and meet them in real life.

The core idea: solve the attention problem for local creatives without Instagram, Facebook, or Spotify. Instead, build an interconnected web of local newsletters, artist portfolios, and community-driven discovery — cooperatively owned by its members, non-commercial, ultra-local (people you can meet on the street), and open-source so other cities can fork it.

**The MVP is newsletter-first:** passionate local people writing about what they love (art galleries, local politics, food spots, dance scenes) on a shared platform that makes writing easy and finding readers effortless. No advertising. No selling. Just community.

I already know about:
- 6AM City (hyperlocal newsletter company, ad-funded, corporate — proof of concept but wrong model for me)
- Platform cooperativism (Trebor Scholz, CoopCycle, Resonate, FairBnB)
- Decidim and Consul (open-source civic software replicated across cities)
- Funkwhale (federated music platform in the Fediverse)
- Subvert (cooperative Bandcamp successor)

I need you to find things I *don't* know yet. Specifically:

## 1. Hyperlocal newsletter platforms and networks

Find platforms, tools, and GitHub repos for:
- Open-source newsletter platforms designed for local/community use (not Substack, not Mailchimp — something community-oriented)
- Federated or cooperative newsletter networks where multiple writers share a platform
- Tools that allow newsletter writers to easily cross-reference each other's content or readers to discover related newsletters
- Any project that combines newsletters with local community discovery
- Ghost (the open-source publishing platform) — has anyone built a cooperative multi-author local newsletter network on top of Ghost?
- Anything resembling "community-owned Substack" or "local newsletter cooperative"

## 2. Ultra-local creative community platforms

Find apps, platforms, or projects that:
- Connect local artists/creators with their neighborhood community (not global platforms)
- Focus on real-life meeting rather than online engagement
- Build interconnected webs of local creatives (artists → musicians → venues → audiences)
- Operate at city or neighborhood scale
- Are cooperative, non-profit, or commons-based
- Include any "local creative directory" or "artist portfolio network" that is community-run
- Specifically look for projects in European cities (Brussels, Amsterdam, Berlin, Barcelona, Lisbon)

## 3. Open-source tools for community-owned social networks

Find GitHub repos and projects building:
- Cooperative or community-owned social network platforms (not Mastodon/ActivityPub general purpose — specifically for local communities)
- Open-source platforms for building local community networks with newsletter + portfolio + discovery features
- Federated community platforms designed to be run independently per city
- Any "Decidim but for culture/community" — open-source civic software applied to arts and local creative scenes
- Bonfire (the modular ActivityPub toolkit) — has anyone used it for local community networks?
- Mobilizon (federated event platform by Framasoft) — how does it work? Is it active?

## 4. Community-driven recommendation and discovery (without algorithms)

Find approaches, tools, or platforms that:
- Help people discover local content/creators without algorithmic feeds
- Use human curation, word-of-mouth mechanics, or social graph (friend-of-friend) rather than AI recommendation
- Build "trust networks" where if you follow one person, you naturally discover their connections
- Any research or writing on "slow social media" or "human-speed networks"
- The concept of "social discovery through trusted intermediaries" — has anyone built this?

## 5. Cooperative and legal structures for community platforms in Europe

Find information about:
- Belgian cooperative structures (SCRL, société coopérative) for digital platforms
- European Cooperative Society (SCE) for cross-border cooperative platforms
- How CoopCycle structured itself legally (federation of cooperatives)
- How Resonate structured its cooperative membership
- Any guide or resource on "how to start a cooperative digital platform in Europe"
- Tax and legal advantages of cooperative structures in Belgium specifically

## Output format

For each finding, give me:
- **Name** of the project, platform, or person
- **What they do** in 1-2 sentences
- **GitHub link** if available
- **Website or key article**
- **Why it matters** for the Grassroots Hopper vision

Prioritize things that are **active, European, community-owned, and actually usable** over theoretical or academic work. If a category has very few results, say so — the gaps tell me what needs to be built.
