# Decidim

## What it is

An open-source participatory democracy platform, born in Barcelona's city government in 2016. Now used by dozens of cities, organizations, and cooperatives worldwide for collective decision-making: proposals, debates, votes, budgets, assemblies.

## What Grassroots Hopper learns from it

**The "forkable city software" model.** Barcelona built it, open-sourced it, and cities worldwide forked it for their own needs. This proves that city-level open-source replication works at scale. Grassroots Hopper wants the exact same dynamic.

**Cooperative governance tools.** Each Grassroots Hopper cooperative needs a place to discuss and decide: where the platform goes, password policies, fee changes, new features, who to hire. Decidim (or Loomio, a lighter alternative) could be plugged in directly as the governance layer.

**Weighted voting.** Decidim supports configurable voting weights and assemblies — relevant for Grassroots Hopper's member class structure (readers ~20%, creators ~40%, event planners ~30%, devs ~10%).

**What to watch out for:** Decidim is powerful but complex. It's built for city-scale participation with thousands of citizens. For a single cooperative of 200 members, it might be overkill. Loomio could be a lighter-weight alternative for early-stage governance.
