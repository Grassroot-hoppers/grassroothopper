# Transition Towns

## What it is

A grassroots movement founded by Rob Hopkins in Totnes, UK in 2006. Communities self-organize around local resilience — energy, food, economy, culture. No central authority. 1,400+ communities in 50+ countries. Spread through handbooks, peer learning, and passionate local leaders.

## What Grassroots Hopper learns from it

**The replication model is the product.** Transition Towns didn't build a platform. They wrote a handbook, trained local initiators, and let communities self-organize. The "product" is the knowledge of how to start and sustain a local initiative. Grassroots Hopper should think the same way — the open-source code is one deliverable, but the handbook for "how to start your own community hub" is equally important.

**Name the identity, then let it spread.** Rob Hopkins named "Transition Towns," gave it a clear identity and set of principles, and then got out of the way. People self-selected into it. The identity did the work. "Grassroots Hopper" as an identity — a type of person, not just a product — follows the same pattern.

**No central authority.** Each Transition Town is independent. There's a Transition Network that provides resources, but no one controls local initiatives. This is exactly the cooperative federation model Grassroots Hopper aims for.

**The inspiration book.** Rob Hopkins' *The Transition Handbook* (2008) and *From What Is to What If* (2019) are foundational texts that gave the movement intellectual weight and emotional pull. Grassroots Hopper could benefit from a similar "book" — even if it's a long-form web document or a beautifully designed PDF.
