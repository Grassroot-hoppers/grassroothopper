# Creative Platforms

Grouped reference models from the creative/cultural platform space. Each proves a piece of the puzzle but none solve the whole problem Grassroot Hopper addresses.

---

## Resonate — Cooperative Music Streaming

**What it is:** A cooperative music streaming platform. Stream-to-own model: you pay per listen, and after ~9 plays you own the track. €10/year membership. Artist-owned governance.

**What we learn:** Proves the cooperative model works for creative platforms. The stream-to-own economic model is inventive. But Resonate struggles with adoption — the network effect problem is brutal for music platforms.

**What to watch out for:** Resonate has been building for years with slow adoption. The cooperative governance model sometimes slows down product decisions. Building a platform that competes with Spotify on features is a losing game.

- Website: https://resonate.coop
- GitHub: https://github.com/resonatecoop

---

## Subvert — Cooperative Bandcamp Successor

**What it is:** Self-described as the "Mondragon of Music" — a cooperative alternative to Bandcamp after its acquisition and layoffs. Artist-owned, zero-commission on sales.

**What we learn:** The cooperative response to corporate acquisition. When Bandcamp was sold and gutted, the community mobilized to build an alternative. This is the energy Grassroot Hopper taps into — people want to own their tools. Subvert's "Mondragon of Music" framing is powerful — naming the ambition clearly.

- Website: https://subvert.fm

---

## Ghost — Open-Source Publishing

**What it is:** An open-source publishing platform for blogs, newsletters, and memberships. Self-hostable. Used by creators and publications worldwide.

**What we learn:** Ghost already solves blogs + newsletters + membership. It could be a technical foundation for Grassroot Hopper's MVP — multi-author blogs behind a membership gate, plus a newsletter system. The no-interaction model means we don't need Bonfire's social features; Ghost's simpler stack might be enough.

**What to watch out for:** Ghost is designed for individual publishers, not community hubs. Adapting it for multi-community, multi-creator use would require significant customization.

- Website: https://ghost.org
- GitHub: https://github.com/TryGhost/Ghost

---

## 6AM City — Proof of Hyperlocal Demand

**What it is:** A network of hyperlocal email newsletters covering 400+ US cities. Nearly 2 million subscribers. Ad-funded, corporate-owned.

**What we learn:** Massive proof that people want ultra-local content about their city. The demand is real. But 6AM City is corporate and ad-funded — the wrong model for community ownership. They proved the appetite; Grassroot Hopper builds the cooperative alternative.

- Website: https://6amcity.com

---

## Cross-cutting takeaways

1. **The demand is proven.** 6AM City's 2M subscribers and Resonate's existence prove people want local creative platforms and cooperative alternatives.
2. **The technology exists.** Ghost, Bonfire, and ActivityPub mean we don't need to build from scratch.
3. **The hard part is adoption, not technology.** Every platform here struggles with getting enough users. Grassroot Hopper's community-first approach (join through a group you're already in) is the answer to this.
4. **Cooperative governance is possible but slow.** Resonate and CoopCycle show it works, but decision-making is harder than in a traditional company. Plan for it.
